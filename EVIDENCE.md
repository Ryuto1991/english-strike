# Evidence Base

## Product claim

English Strike should train business-English survival through a daily loop of:

1. forced recall
2. immediate feedback
3. spoken repetition
4. spaced review
5. task-grounded reuse

## Why these mechanisms are in the product

### Retrieval practice

Retrieval practice is stronger than passive restudy for retention.
In foreign-language digital flashcard learning, retrieval practice outperformed restudying and remained robust across test modalities.

Source:

- Huang et al. (2025), PubMed: [The Moderating Role of Learning Rounds: Effects on Retrieval Practice and Context Dependence in Digital Flashcard Foreign Language Vocabulary Learning](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41301342/)

### Repetition and spacing

Repeated encounters have a medium positive relationship with incidental L2 vocabulary learning, and effects vary with spacing and engagement.

Source:

- Uchihara, Webb, & Yanagisawa (2019): [The Effects of Repetition on Incidental Vocabulary Learning: A Meta-Analysis of Correlational Studies](https://tus.elsevierpure.com/en/publications/the-effects-of-repetition-on-incidental-vocabulary-learning-a-met)

### Dialogue-based speaking practice

Dialogue-based CALL systems show a positive effect on L2 speaking development.
This supports work-task grounded output practice rather than passive study alone.

Source:

- Hou & Min (2025): [Dialogue-based computer-assisted language learning systems for second language speaking development: A three-level meta-analysis](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/recall/article/dialoguebased-computerassisted-language-learning-systems-for-second-language-speaking-development-a-threelevel-metaanalysis/31847710516602398819C5E594038E7B)

### Pronunciation support

Pronunciation-support systems are useful when they provide active, feedback-oriented practice.
This supports making read-aloud a required step, but not the whole product.

Source:

- Amrate & Tsai (2024): [Computer-assisted pronunciation training: A systematic review](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/recall/article/computerassisted-pronunciation-training-a-systematic-review/71E786F7DFC99727837909FDED7A2320)

## Inference boundary

The product uses evidence-informed design.
Not every feature is directly prescribed by a single paper.

The following are product judgments:

- the exact `1 / 3 / 7` review loop
- business-English task categories
- the 12-sentence first assignment
- the requirement to track real-world reuse
